Concept Overview · 2025 Pilot

AI-Powered Support for
Charities & Small Businesses

How one volunteer with AI tools replaced weeks of manual work — and what that model could look like at scale.

Piloted at Christ Presbyterian Church · Madison International Partners Silent Auction 2025

❗The Problem

Nonprofits and small businesses have real operational needs — but almost never have the technical resources to meet them.

👥

No IT Staff

Most charities run on volunteers, many retired or non-technical. Professional tech help is unaffordable.

⏱️

Manual, Repetitive Work

Data entry, donor tracking, website updates, and event logistics eat dozens of volunteer hours every year.

💸

Tight Budgets

Software subscriptions, web agencies, and designers are luxuries most nonprofits simply cannot afford.

🔄

Repeated Every Year

Annual events like silent auctions redo the same work from scratch because nothing is systematized or reusable.

🧪The Pilot — Madison Silent Auction 2025

One volunteer with AI tools built what previously required a committee plus outside help.

What was built in a single session, using only AI:

  • A fully designed, mobile-friendly auction catalog website with search, category filters, item detail popups, and Google Maps integration — customized for the church and event
  • A bid sheet scanning tool that reads photos of handwritten bid sheets and extracts winners automatically — handling cursive, mixed printing, and messy handwriting
  • A donor/contact tracking system structured for future reuse, replacing a manually maintained Excel spreadsheet
  • A sponsor acknowledgment banner and branded header — no design skills required

❌ Before AI

  • Website: Not done (too costly / complex)
  • Item catalog: Printed paper list
  • Winner tallying: Manual, stressful, error-prone
  • Donor tracking: Loose spreadsheet per year
  • Setup time: 20–40 volunteer hours
  • Tech help: Depended on one person who might not return

✅ With AI

  • Website: Done, live, shareable in hours
  • Item catalog: Searchable, mobile-friendly, filterable
  • Winner tallying: Photo → winners list in under 2 minutes
  • Donor tracking: Structured, reusable year over year
  • Setup time: 2–4 hours (including learning)
  • Tech help: Any literate volunteer can operate it

🤖What AI Actually Does Here

Each task below used to require specialized skills. AI makes them accessible to any motivated volunteer or small business owner.

🌐

Instant Websites

Describe your event or business, and AI generates a complete, branded, mobile-friendly website — no coding or design skills needed.

Saves 15–40 hours
✍️

Handwriting Recognition

Photo of a handwritten bid sheet → AI reads the cursive, identifies the highest bidder, and produces a winner list in seconds.

Saves 30–60 min on auction night
📋

Data Entry & Organization

Upload a donor list, spreadsheet, or stack of cards. AI structures, cleans, categorizes, and prepares data for reuse next year.

Saves 5–10 hours
✉️

Communications

Donor thank-you letters, business solicitation emails, event announcements, social media posts — drafted and personalized in minutes.

Saves 3–8 hours
📊

Reports & Tracking

Turn raw data into structured reports — funds raised, top bidders, donor retention trends — without any spreadsheet expertise.

Saves 2–5 hours
🎨

Design & Materials

Bid sheets, event programs, sponsor flyers, name badges, and signage — generated from a description, ready to print.

Saves 4–12 hours

💡Potential Business Model

Three ways this pilot could become a repeatable service offering for other organizations.

Option A

Volunteer-as-a-Service

One AI-fluent volunteer is placed with a charity for their annual event cycle. They use AI tools to do the work of 5–10 traditional volunteers — then hand off a repeatable system for next year.

Free or cost of AI tools (~$20–50/mo)

  • Ideal for: churches, PTAs, community nonprofits
  • Risk: relies on finding volunteers
  • Outcome: sustainable, lower cost every year
Option B

Micro-Consultancy

Offer a fixed-price "event-in-a-box" package to small nonprofits: website, bid sheets, donor tracking, post-event reports, thank-you letters. Delivered in 1–2 weeks using AI tools.

$300–800 per event (per engagement)

  • Ideal for: charities without any tech volunteers
  • Risk: requires marketing / client acquisition
  • Outcome: revenue-generating, scalable to 10–20 events/yr
Option C

Nonprofit Tech Cooperative

A small group (2–4 people) forms a shared services cooperative for 10–15 local nonprofits. Each nonprofit pays a small annual retainer in exchange for on-demand AI-assisted support throughout the year.

$500–1,500/org/year (retainer model)

  • Ideal for: established local nonprofit community
  • Risk: requires trust-building and coordination
  • Outcome: recurring revenue, deep impact, community anchor
Key insight: The AI tools cost roughly $20–50/month. The value delivered — websites, scanning tools, donor systems, communications — would cost a nonprofit $3,000–15,000 if purchased from traditional vendors. The opportunity is the translation layer between AI capability and organizations that need it but can't access it on their own.

🗺️Suggested Next Steps

From this pilot to a repeatable model — a phased approach.

1
Now — Complete the 2025 Pilot

Document what worked

Run the 2025 auction with these AI tools. Capture time saved, volunteer reactions, and any friction points. This becomes your proof-of-concept story.

2
Post-event — Systematize

Build a repeatable "playbook"

Turn the tools and process into a simple guide any volunteer can follow next year. The goal: the 2026 auction should take half the time the 2025 auction took.

3
Fall 2025 — First External Pilot

Offer it to one other local nonprofit — free

Find a sister organization running a similar event. Offer to set up their auction tools at no cost. Use it to test whether the playbook transfers to a new organization without hand-holding.

4
2026 — Decide on the model

Choose your path and begin client acquisition

Based on the external pilot experience, choose Option A (volunteer), B (consultancy), or C (cooperative). Begin outreach to 3–5 local nonprofits with a concrete service offer and pricing.
